How it works

1Share the space as it is

Take a few photos, exactly as it looks today. No tidying up first, and nothing to measure unless you want sharper product suggestions.

2Review a plan made for your household

TidyMap reads what’s on your shelves, then drafts where everything should live around your routines, your kids and pets, and your budget. You confirm what it found before the plan is built.

3Work through it one manageable step at a time

Each step is short and timed. Pause for school pickup, come back after bedtime, and the plan holds together.

What we promise

We start with what you own.

Every plan begins at $0, built on your existing shelves, bins, and baskets.

Safety is planned in.

With kids or pets in the house, hazards go up high or behind a latch, and their own things stay reachable.

Purchases are always optional.

Product suggestions stay off unless you ask for them, and the plan works without them.

Your plan can be adjusted.

Change a zone, lower the effort, or swap a step. It’s your space; the plan follows.

Who’s behind this

TidyMap is built by SCM Solutions, a small team, and is currently in early access. We started it after watching capable, busy households lose the same twenty minutes every day to spaces that had quietly stopped working.

The organizing rules behind every plan are ordinary professional practice: things you use daily live at eye level, heavy items stay low, kid items stay reachable, and hazards don’t. The AI’s job is narrower than you might expect.

What the AI does

It reads your photos to catalog what’s actually on your shelves and how much room you have, then applies the placement rules above.

What it doesn’t do

It doesn’t judge, doesn’t sell your data, and doesn’t invent products. Suggestions come from a hand-checked list of real retailer items.

How photos are handled

Photos are used to build your plan. If you don’t sign in, they aren’t stored after the analysis. Details in the privacy policy.

Early access

TidyMap is free while we finish building it. Some rough edges remain; the feedback form at the end of every plan goes straight to us.
